1.HEAD ACHE



Headache is a very common ailment. The main reason is the fallout of sensitive cells and nerve fibers,
mainly located inside and outside the placenta. Headaches are caused by many eye diseases.
These pains occur only after continuous reading and prolonged eye contact. If the headache
persists for a long time, it should be treated in a timely manner. Pneumonia, Dental Diseases, ear infections,
depression, constipation and flatulence are some of the common causes for headaches.

3.Crush root of snake gourd and apply in forehead.
4.Wet cotton in beetroot juice and keep it in ears.





2.DIZZINESS

This is also a major cause of dizziness.

3.EPILEPSY



The main cause of epilepsy is an abnormal electrical discharge from certain parts of the brain. Headache,
loss of appetite, abdominal cramps, car cramps. With symptoms such as bite to the ear and swelling of the ears.
Foaming at the mouth. These are the symptoms of epilepsy that usually occur within five minutes and do not last
long. However, in some serious cases it can take a long time. In some, it keeps coming back.



REMEDIES:



1.Drink ripe ash gourd juice mixed with jaggery.
2.Mix brahmi with hot milk and drink .

4.INSOMNIA



These include insomnia, which makes it difficult to fall asleep, and occasional waking up too
early after a good night's sleep. Vigilant views, sounds, and thoughts are annoying to such patients.
The main causes of insomnia are mental disorders, indigestion, excruciating pain, high fever, high blood pressure,
anemia, respiratory patients with dementia, depression and severe hydrocephalus.



REMEDIES:



1.Eat fashion fruit just before bed.
2.Crush Thottavadi root in water and drink it in the evening and half a glass before going to bed.
3.Roast the skin of wormwood and apply it on the feet at night.
4.Drink a glass of sugarcane juice, green gooseberry juice. Add sugar to a glass of hot milk and drink it
before going to bed at night.





5.PARKINSONS



It is most common in people between the ages of 50 and 60. Start first on the tops of the legs or arms.
Then spreads downwards. Sometimes it affects the tongue and eyelids. There will be trembling numbness and
difficulty in moving that body part. Although there is no barrier to speaking, speech can often be fast and confusing.



REMEDIES:



1.Practice meditation.
2.Infusion of Anakurunthotti root and powdered cheruthippali can be taken on an empty stomach
in the morning and in the evening.
3.Amalpori root, Brahmi, Kilithinipanji, Karimkurunjivar, Chethi and Sapushpam should be taken in equal
proportions and drunk.





6.HEMIPLEGIA



The blood delivers nutrients and oxygen to the brain. When blood does not reach any part of the brain,
permanent damage to the cells in that area occurs. It sometimes paralyzes a part of the body.
In hypertensive patients, high blood pressure causes the tiny arteries inside the brain to rupture.
The blood that flows out in this way damages the nearby cells of those arteries. This also causes paralysis
in the major arteries that supply blood to the brain.
