MALNUTRICIOUS DISEASES

1.ANEMIA



Anemia signs and symptoms vary depending on the cause. If the anemia is caused by a chronic disease,
the disease can mask them, so that the anemia might be detected by tests for another condition.
Depending on the causes of your anemia, you might have no symptoms. Signs and symptoms, if they do occur,
might include:
Fatigue, Weakness, Pale or yellowish skin, Irregular heartbeats, Shortness of breath, Dizziness or lightheadedness, Chest pain, Cold hands and feet, Headaches

1.Shred the coconut in the coriander leaves and cook and eat it.
2.Dry the neem flower and eat it in ghee.
3.Add pepper in boiled milk and drink.
4.Take keezharnelli and mix it with cow's milk and drink .
5.Eat lots of fruits milk and barley pictures.





2. RICKETS



The symptoms of this disease are the appearance of powdery spots all over the body between the joints,
the nails and the gums, with a lot of blood flowing from the small blood vessels. These patients regularly
experience anemic lethargy and pain in the hands and feet.



REMEDIES:



1.Take a green frog and take the flesh of the leg and boil it in coconut milk and give it in the morning
and night You can also drink soup. 2.Consumption of fruits and milk in large quantities can reduce the risk of developing the disease.
3.It is a disease caused by a deficiency of vitamin C. Eat grapes, oranges, tomatoes, pomegranates, papaya and apples.





3. BERI BERI



It is a disease caused by a deficiency of thiamine (B 1) vitamin. It is an integral part of the proper
functioning of the heart, nervous system, and intestines.



REMEDIES:



1.People with this disease should regularly eat a mixture of rice bran and palm jaggery.
2.In addition milk,fruits, vegetables and eggs should be used.





4.KWASHIORKOR



Processes such as organ growth and enzyme production naturally slow down. Infected children develop
dehydration. There will not be enough weight according to height. The skin is dry and cracked.
Swelling of the liver. The muscles near the chest and shoulders become weak. The hair is thin and coppery.
There will be lethargy and fatigue.



REMEDIES:



1.Consume plenty of milk, butter, ghee, fish oil, vegetable soups and fruit juices.





5.MARASMUS



Children who suffer from premature aging are more likely to have marasmus. Their skin is wrinkled
and their body looks emaciated. The main cause of the disease is malnutrition. The disease usually
develops between the ages of six months and two years after the baby is born.



REMEDIES:



1.Rinse the head regularly with boiled water with gooseberry.
2.Add a lot of curry leaves in coconut oil and rub it.
3.Roast the henna leaves in sand and then in coconut oil.
4.Dilute and apply.
5.Sprinkle curry leaves on the scalp for 20 minutes. Apply black cumin on the scalp.
6.Add 20 gms of the mixture mixed with chopped shallots daily.
7.Mix curry peel, gooseberry, melabi, cayenne and aloe vera and apply on hair. After one hour,
take a bath. Mix equal amount of coconut oil and almond oil and apply on scalp.
8.Bathe the head with black tea.





6.OBESITY



The disease is seen with age. Overeating is the cause of obesity. The disease can be identified by
looking at the infected person. They will be overweight. They will lose their limbs. Life expectancy
will decrease. Pain, arthritis, arthritis, paralysis, high blood pressure and infertility are common in
these patients. In addition, being overweight can put a strain on the heart and lead to heart disease.
The disease is also hereditary.The main REMEDIES for this disease is to reduce food intake and increase
exercise.
